▍ Why Subversion, why here

The model that makes large, linear, auditable work legible.

svn·hub leans into what SVN is genuinely good at and shows it directly, rather than hiding it behind git-shaped metaphors.

Global revision numbers

A single monotonic counter: r128 means the exact same tree to everyone, forever. No relative refs, no "ahead by 3 of who?"

One central source of truth

The repository is the canonical state. There is no distributed ambiguity to reconcile. What is on the server is what is.

Partial, path-based checkouts

Check out a single directory with svn co ^/trunk/docs and leave gigabytes of history you do not need on the server.

Atomic, ordered commits

A commit is one transaction across many files at one revision. It either all lands or none of it does. No half states, ever.

First-class branches & tags

They're just directories (/branches, /tags) made by cheap server-side copy. Addressable, browsable, obvious.

Working-copy insight

See who has checked out and how far each copy has drifted from HEAD, including the freshness number Git-style hosting cannot show.

▍ The honest comparison

A different contract, not a worse one.

Git: distributed, content-addressed

  • SHA-1 hashes are opaque and non-sequential
  • Clones the entire history locally
  • Merge-first; conflicts are routine
  • Branches/refs live in a hidden namespace

Subversion: centralized, path-addressed

  • Sequential r1, r2, r3… everyone shares
  • Check out only the paths you touch
  • Server is the single source of truth
  • Branches & tags are real directories
▍ Why this service exists

Not every team needs a fork-heavy, distributed workflow.

The longer explanation is simple: Git is excellent for some projects, but Subversion is still a better fit when authority, auditability, and practical visibility matter more than local history gymnastics.

01

A better fit for many projects

Internal business applications, client projects, regulated systems, small teams, centrally managed repositories, and many private software projects often benefit from a single authoritative source of truth.

With Subversion, there is one canonical repository, one official project history, and a simple sequence of globally ordered revision numbers. Users update, make changes, and commit. The repository moves forward in a clear, understandable way.

For teams that care about audit trails, accountability, historical integrity, and straightforward reconstruction of prior system states, Subversion remains a rational choice.

02

Better statistics and visibility

A centralized repository model makes it possible to show activity by user, repository history, revision progress, checkout behavior, update behavior, working-copy freshness, and how far users are behind HEAD.

Those statistics are not decorative. They help teams spot stale work, identify coordination problems, and understand what is happening in their projects.

03

Independent, practical, and free

svn·hub is designed for users who want control over their development infrastructure without forcing every project into one corporate ecosystem.

Free access keeps source hosting available to individuals, small teams, students, hobbyists, businesses, and organizations without unnecessary barriers.

04

Modern web features around a proven system

Subversion is mature, stable, and proven. svn·hub adds the web conveniences people expect: repository browsing, history, diffs, issue tracking, merge requests, project visibility, and useful statistics.

The result is not nostalgia for an older tool. It is a modern application built around a version-control model that still makes sense for many real-world projects.
